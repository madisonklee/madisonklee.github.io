Fragrance World

A website to democratize fragrances, where brands can upload their own perfumes and link users on locations to purchase and users can upload their own reviews. It's the HuggingFace of perfumes and colognes.

Role:Sole designer & engineer - product framing, UX, frontend, backend, and deployment

In Progress

Problem Framing

The Discovery

I got into perfumes about a year and a half ago, but I've never been someone who overconsumes. I found myself constantly smelling perfumes in stores but rarely buying because I didn't want to spend $150 on just any scent. This led me to discover smaller indie brands-some with unique fragrances, some offering dupes of high-end perfumes.

The Market Gap

As I dove deeper into the perfume world, I realized there's a vibrant community of enthusiasts and a vast number of small brands that few people have ever heard of. From an AI and tech perspective, I saw an inefficiency: these small brands were going undiscovered, users didn't have a central place to explore and share insights, and the market was fragmented.

The Vision

I set out to build the "Hugging Face of perfumes"-a platform where brands can upload their own information and users can easily search for and discover fragrances. Existing databases like Fragrantica and Parfumo didn't give brands control over their listings and often prioritized outdated interfaces over user experience. The problem was clear: small brands lacked visibility, users lacked control, and the market was inefficient.

Constraint

I was the only "frag-head" technologist I knew, meaning I had to build this entirely solo.

Product & Design Thinking

User Research & Insights

To build a platform that truly helped fragrance enthusiasts, I spoke with friends and members of the fragrance community. Their needs ranged from simply wanting to know a perfume's notes to wanting a comprehensive, all-in-one platform.

Casual users

Most didn't even know fragrance databases existed. The key was to offer them a simple, intuitive experience.

Enthusiast users

Already active on other platforms, they needed reasons to switch-access to small indie brands, direct communication with brands, discovery tools, and a better, ad-free experience.

Small brands

These brands had never had a voice on a democratized platform. The challenge was making the platform simple enough for non-technical users while providing real exposure.

Large brands

This would be the hardest group to engage, so my strategy was to focus on user and small brand traction first before targeting them.

Execution (Design to Build)

Design

I iterated multiple times to achieve a look that was chic, playful, and easy to understand. Every feature had to be intuitive and functional, following the principle that there is beauty when something works and it works intuitively.

Engineering

I built the platform using Next.js, connecting front- and back-end seamlessly, and hosted the database on Google Cloud. This setup allowed me to scale while keeping the system maintainable.

Technical Architecture

Fragrance World is a full-stack JavaScript application:

Framework

Next.js 15.5.3 with React 19.1.0 for the frontend UI

Database

PostgreSQL, with custom queries via the pg library to manage relationships between perfumes, brands, users, reviews, and analytics

Authentication

JWT tokens stored in local/session storage, with password hashing via Node.js crypto module

Frontend

Component-based architecture using CSS modules and inline styles, Google Fonts, and a responsive design system with CSS variables

Architecture Pattern

The application uses Next.js Pages Router with API routes handling backend logic, creating a monolithic but organized codebase where serverless endpoints and frontend pages coexist.
