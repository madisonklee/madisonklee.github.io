Recovery

Recovery is a home-based therapy app for stroke patients with apraxia or aphasia, enabling them to practice speech and occupational exercises with guided speech-movement examples.

Role:Sole designer & engineer - product framing, UX, frontend, backend, and deployment

Complete

Problem Framing

The Discovery

My mom had a stroke 10 years ago, and I saw firsthand how difficult it was to find apps that addressed her specific needs. Existing stroke recovery apps were limited, full of ads, and often confusing to follow. I realized there was an opportunity to create a better, patient-centered solutionâ€”one designed with real users and their caregivers in mind. This project was developed alongside my mom's ongoing feedback.

The Market Gap

Most existing apps for stroke recovery:

  • Focus on generic therapy exercises, not tailored to patients with aphasia or apraxia
  • Are filled with ads, overwhelming visuals, and poor UX
  • Lack guided speech-movement examples to help patients practice accurately at home

The Vision

I set out to build an intuitive, ad-free app that enables stroke patients to:

  • Practice speech and movement exercises at their own pace
  • Follow guided, step-by-step examples for therapy
  • Feel empowered and supported in their recovery journey

Constraint

I wasn't sure how to create speech-guided movement exercises without recording actual audio/video. (Today, AI tools could easily solve this challenge.)

Product & Design Thinking

User Segments

Stroke Patients (Apraxia/Aphasia)

Need clear, guided exercises that are easy to follow and repeat independently.

Caregivers/Family Members

Need a reliable tool to support their loved ones' recovery at home.

Therapists

Looking for a digital companion to reinforce exercises outside the clinic.

Research & Insights

Observed how patients struggled with confusing app flows

Conducted feedback sessions with my mom on what motivates engagement and practice consistency

Identified that simple, guided interactions with immediate feedback are critical for adoption

Execution (Design to Build)

Design

Created multiple UX/UI iterations focused on clarity, accessibility, and empathy

Developed visual language using large buttons, simple navigation, and color coding for exercise types

Designed guided speech-movement flows to combine audio prompts and visual demonstrations

Engineering

Not fully implemented, but prototype design is ready for integration

AI-enabled speech guidance could now replace the initial recording constraint

Impact

Patient-Focused Design

Early, patient-focused design ensures that technology meets real-world needs.

UX Clarity

UX clarity and simplicity are critical for accessibility in healthcare apps.

Personal Motivation

Personal motivation and family involvement are powerful drivers for adoption and engagement.

Learnings & Tradeoffs

Technical Challenge

The technical challenge of implementing guided speech-movement exercises without recordings or AI support.

Marketing/Challenges

Reaching stroke patients and caregivers is complex; offline communities and therapists are key channels.

Respect Gained

Deepened appreciation for therapists, caregivers, and patients who navigate daily rehabilitation challenges.
